Rules & Scoring

The game

A pick'em bracket for the 2026 FIFA World Cup. Predict who advances through every round, earn points for getting it right, and ride the leaderboard from June 11 to the final on July 19.

How to play

  1. Pick your name (or add yourself) on the home page.
  2. For each of the 12 groups, rank all 4 teams from 1st to 4th by tapping two teams to swap their positions. Only your 1st and 2nd score group-stage points; your 3rd determines wildcard eligibility.
  3. Pick exactly 8 of the 12 third-place teams to advance as wildcards. The R32 bracket auto-populates from FIFA's official Annexe C lookup table.
  4. Click winners through R32 → R16 → QF → SF → Final, plus the 3rd-place match.
  5. Your champion is whoever you pick to win the Final. In the tiebreaker, predict that champion's average goals per game across the whole tournament.
  6. Click Save my picks as you go (commits your current draft to the database). Click Submit when you're done — finalizes everything until you click Edit.

Deadlines

Everything locks at the first kickoff: Thursday, June 11, 2026 — 1:00 PM Mexico City time (Mexico vs South Africa, Estadio Azteca).

After kickoff, no more edits. Everyone's picks become visible, and the leaderboard goes live.

How the bracket cascade works

The bracket is one continuous tree. After R32 winners are picked, R16 slots auto-populate with whichever teams you advanced. R16 winners feed QF, QF feeds SF, SF feeds the Final (and the SF losers play for 3rd place).

If you change an R32 team partway through, downstream slots update automatically. Any winner pick whose team is no longer in its match is quietly dropped — you'll need to re-pick that match.

Save vs. Submit

Your clicks live in a draft until you commit them:

  • Save my picks writes your current draft to the database. You can keep editing afterward.
  • Submit writes your draft AND marks your picks as final. Editing is disabled until you click Edit picks to unlock again.
  • Both Save and Submit are no-ops if your draft already matches the database.
  • Leaving the site (close tab, refresh, click Rules) with unsaved changes triggers a warning. The custom modal on internal links offers a "Save & continue" or "Submit & continue" shortcut.

The tiebreaker, explained

Your champion is whichever team you've picked to win the Final — you don't choose them separately. The tiebreaker number is your prediction of that champion's average goals per game across every match they play (group stage + knockouts).

If two players finish tied on points, whoever's prediction is closest to the real tournament average for the actual champion wins the tiebreaker. The team you picked doesn't have to be the team that actually wins — we always compare against reality.

Decimals are fine: 2.3, 1.75, etc. A typical World Cup winner averages somewhere between 1.5 and 2.5 goals per game.

Visibility

Until first kickoff on June 11, only your own picks are visible to you. No peeking at how other players are filling their brackets. At kickoff, everything is revealed and the leaderboard goes live.

Picks model: details

  • Group ranks: tap a team to highlight it, then tap a second team in the same group to swap their positions. Tap the same team twice to deselect. The default order is FIFA pot order until you make your first swap.
  • Auto-fill: shuffles all four teams in each empty group so the top three become a random 1st/2nd/3rd. Groups you've already touched are left alone.
  • Wildcards: after every group is ranked, pick exactly 8 of the 12 third-place teams. The R32 matchups are looked up from FIFA's official Annexe C table — you don't place teams into slots yourself.
  • Winner picks: click a team in a match to advance them; click again to un-pick.
  • Exact-score bonuses: optional. Predict 2-1, 3-0, etc. for any knockout match. Earn +2 if your score matches the real final score exactly. (UI coming soon.)

How results get entered

The site fetches match results automatically every 30 minutes from a public data source. If that ever falls behind or breaks, an admin can enter scores manually so the leaderboard stays current.
