Rules & Scoring

The game

A pick'em bracket for the 2026 FIFA World Cup. Predict who advances through every round, earn points for getting it right, and ride the leaderboard from June 11 to the final on July 19.

How to play

  1. Pick your name (or add yourself) on the home page.
  2. For each of the 12 groups, predict who finishes 1st and 2nd.
  3. Fill the 32 Round of 32 slots with any teams you want. The eight "best 3rd-place" wildcards are implicit — drop in whichever teams you think will sneak through.
  4. Click winners through R32 → R16 → QF → SF → Final, plus the 3rd-place match.
  5. Enter your tiebreaker: how many goals will the eventual champion score across the entire tournament?
  6. Optionally, predict exact final scores for any knockout match for bonus points.
  7. Click Save my picks as you go (commits your current draft to the database). Click Submit when you're done — finalizes everything until you click Edit.

Deadlines

Everything locks at the first kickoff: Thursday, June 11, 2026 — 1:00 PM Mexico City time (Mexico vs South Africa, Estadio Azteca).

After kickoff, no more edits. Everyone's picks become visible, and the leaderboard goes live.

How the bracket cascade works

The bracket is one continuous tree. After R32 winners are picked, R16 slots auto-populate with whichever teams you advanced. R16 winners feed QF, QF feeds SF, SF feeds the Final (and the SF losers play for 3rd place).

If you change an R32 team partway through, downstream slots update automatically. Any winner pick whose team is no longer in its match is quietly dropped — you'll need to re-pick that match.

Save vs. Submit

Your clicks live in a draft until you commit them:

  • Save my picks writes your current draft to the database. You can keep editing afterward.
  • Submit writes your draft AND marks your picks as final. Editing is disabled until you click Edit picks to unlock again.
  • Both Save and Submit are no-ops if your draft already matches the database.
  • Leaving the site (close tab, refresh, click Rules) with unsaved changes triggers a warning. The custom modal on internal links offers a "Save & continue" or "Submit & continue" shortcut.

The tiebreaker, explained

If two players finish on equal points, the player whose tiebreaker number is closest to the actual champion's total tournament goals wins.

One key thing: the prediction is about the real champion, not the team you picked to win. If you predict "Brazil will win and score 22 goals" but Argentina actually lifts the trophy with 14 goals, your tiebreaker number (22) is compared against 14 (off by 8). Pick a realistic number for whoever you think wins.

Visibility

Until first kickoff on June 11, only your own picks are visible to you. No peeking at how other players are filling their brackets. At kickoff, everything is revealed and the leaderboard goes live.

Picks model: details

  • Group picks: save partial progress (just 1st without 2nd) at any time.
  • R32 draft: each team can only occupy one R32 slot — picking a team already placed elsewhere moves it.
  • Winner picks: click a team in a match to advance them; click again to un-pick.
  • Exact-score bonuses: optional. Predict 2-1, 3-0, etc. for any knockout match. Earn +2 if your score matches the real final score exactly.

How results get entered

The site fetches match results automatically every 30 minutes from a public data source. If that ever falls behind or breaks, an admin can enter scores manually so the leaderboard stays current.
